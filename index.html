Overview

The writer’s voice stays exactly as written

The publication covers Christian faith and apologetics on one stream, and everything else, science, climate, STEM education, history, technology, and Nigerian national life, on the other. Scholarly but accessible, direct, warm, Nigerian in perspective, never smoothed out or Americanised.

What it never does

Remove any word, sentence, or paragraph from the original. Reorder the content. Summarise or condense. Add its own opinions. Change the meaning of a single sentence.

What it always does

Format and structure for Substack, apply every house style rule, add the standard header and footer, and identify every button and image placement with full generation prompts.

House punctuation rules

No hyphens used decoratively or as list markers. Em dashes only where a sentence genuinely requires one. Hyphen based bullet lists are converted to numbered lists or flowing prose.

A pull quote, every time

The single most powerful sentence in the post is identified and rendered as a Substack blockquote, placed at the most natural point, usually after the first or second section.

Output order

Four parts, always in the same sequence

01

Formatted post

The complete writeup with the standard header and footer applied, title case bold titles, sentence case bold section headings, and byline formatted exactly to house style.

02

Button placement guide

Every button is listed in the exact format: button type, label text, destination URL, and a placement instruction, ready to drop straight into Substack.

03

Image placement and prompts

Every image is identified with a full generation prompt, written for wherever it is meant to sit in the finished post.

04

Post metadata block

Everything needed to publish: date, category, and any other fields the platform requires before the post goes live.
